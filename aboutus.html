Scrimshaw Museum

About Us

Founded in 2026, the Scrimshaw Museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the rich history and artistry of scrimshaw, a traditional form of ivory carving practiced by sailors and whalers in the 18th and 19th centuries.

Our Mission

The mission of the Scrimshaw Museum is to preserve maritime folk art and American history through its collection of scrimshaw. We strive to engage the public with a deeper understanding and appreciation of the self-taught artistic talents of scrimshanders in efforts to ensure the artform is not lost to time.

Values and Vision

The Scrimshaw Museum envisions a compassionate future where our visitors value maritime art and respect the lives of animals used to create it. The values of the Museum align with the values of the Endangered Species Act, the Marine Mammal Protection Act, and the Native American Graves Protection and Repatriation Act. We maintain an ethically and legally sourced collection of scrimshaw for the value of education and preservation of the art of scrimshaw.

Common Questions About Our Collection

Is the Scrimshaw Museum's collection ethically sourced?

Yes, the Scrimshaw Museum maintains an ethically and legally sourced collection of scrimshaw for the value of education and preservation. Because a large portion of our collection consists of donated objects, most of our objects contain foax ivory.

Can I Ask for the Provenance of an Object?

Yes, you can request information about the provenance of any object in our collection if our labels do not provide sufficient details.

Can I donate scrimshaw pieces to the museum?

Yes, the Scrimshaw Museum accepts donations of scrimshaw pieces with proper documentation for its collection.
