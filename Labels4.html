Powder Horn

Materials: Leather, Horn, Wood, Metal

Artist: James Eastman

Date: 2003

Horn was the ideal material for storing gunpowder as it was naturally waterproof and spark resistant. Engraved power horns became a popular practice in the 18th century during the French Indian War. Like Nantucket whalers and lightshipman, soldiers spent their prolonged periods of waiting craving horns with needles and small knives. The image would become visible after the engraver would rub grease, wax, or ash into the fine grooves. Eastman’s modern interpretation showcases the enduring legacy of scrimshaw.
