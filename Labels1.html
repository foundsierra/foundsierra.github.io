Nantucket Basket

Materials: Wood, Rattan, Leather, Plastic

Lightship basket weaving has been part of Nantucket culture dating back to the mid 1800s. Men stationed aboard lightships occupied their leisure time by weaving ovular rattan baskets that would eventually be sold in shops on shore. In 1916, the US government required that lightship basket making would be created ashore to prevent moonlighting. After World War II, Nantucketer José Formoso Reyes, developed the “Friendship Basket” which added carved ornaments to the lid. As demand and interest in the baskets continued to grow, the baskets would begin to be mass produced in China beginning in the 1970s.
